### The Convergence Stall on Stiff Systems

When solving continuous differential equations with Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), boundary constraints and domain conservation metrics often conflict. Standard optimizers like Adam treat these loss components as independent scalars, leading to limit-cycle oscillations in the parameter updates.

SCIO resolves this stiffness mathematically by analyzing the local Hessian eigenspectrum. By computing diagonal pre-conditioning coefficients, the optimizer scales gradient updates inversely proportional to local curvature:

$$
\theta_{t+1} = \theta_t - \eta P_t \nabla L(\theta_t) \quad \text{where} \quad P_t = \text{diag}(\max(\lambda_i, \epsilon))^{-1}
$$

### Experimental Results on Allen-Cahn

We validated SCIO on the highly stiff 1D Allen-Cahn equation with phase-separation scaling $\epsilon = 0.001$. Where Adam stalls, SCIO achieves stable convergence in under 2,500 epochs:

| Metric | Adam Optimizer | SCIO Optimizer (Ours) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Epochs to Converge** | Stalls (50,000+) | **2,500** (15x speedup) |
| **Final L2 Relative Error** | $2.3 \times 10^{-1}$ | **$1.2 \times 10^{-4}$** |
| **Computation Cost / Epoch** | $1.0\times$ | $1.15\times$ |

This represents a substantial step forward for automated parameter search in scientific machine learning pipelines.
